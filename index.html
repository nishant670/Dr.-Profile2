About Dr. Manik

Dr. Manik graduated from Pt. Ravishankar Shukla University, Raipur and after completing his postdoctoral qualification (DM) in Cardiology from Maulana Azad Medical College & GB Pant Hospital, New Delhi, he underwent Fellowship in Interventional Cardiology & Electrophysiology from St George Hospital, London & France. He holds MBA in Healthcare Administration from Faculty of Management Studies, Delhi University. He is a fellow of International Medical Sciences Academy (FIMSA), Society for Cardiovascular Angiography & Intervention (FSCAI) & Fellow of the American College of Cardiology (FACC). His primary area of interest has cardiac arrhythmias and cardiac interventions. He has published more than 75 papers in Interventional Cardiology, EP & Pacing and has been Author & Co-author of many cardiology books.

Surviving a heart attack: a Non-STEMI

2 July, 2017

Surviving a heart attack: what does it mean for a patient to have survived a non-STEMI? Non-STEMI or Non-ST elevation Myocardial Infarction is the dominant presentation of acute coronary syndrome. This occurs when the blood supplied to the heart muscle is suddenly blocked. Other presentations include unstable angina and STEMI or ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction. Patients presenting with symptoms of chest pain with or without accompanying symptoms of shortness of breath, profuse sweating, nausea or syncope, and have evidence of myocardial necrosis (elevation of cardiac enzyme: troponin) with absence of ST elevation are considered to have a Non-STEMI.

Interventional Management of Resistant Hypertension

15 April, 2017

Renal denervation (RD) is a minimally invasive percutaneous approach that involves placement of an electrode in the renal arteries followed by ablation of sympathetic nerves. Sympathetic innervations stimulates renin release from the juxtoglomerular apparatus and afferent vasoconstriction of renal vessels, both of which lead to increased tubular reabsorption of sodium and elevated blood pressure. Renal denervation is therefore based on the principle that deactivation of renal sympathetic nerves will curb hypertension.
